## Doctrine — how we "come correct"
Don't ship guesses. Before ANY content/strategy decision (posting times, clip length, format,
hook, cadence), ground it in three things, in order:
1. **Current research** — this doc + a fresh web pull if it's stale or the call is high-stakes. Cite sources.
2. **Live data via the closed loop** — scoreboard → optimize → retention curves (`swipe_away_pct`) → `by_hour` timing → A/B winners. The loop tunes specifics; you start from evidence.
3. **RT's hook framework** — `config/hook-playbook.md` (Undertow: 16 principles + 13 formulas from 1,153 analyzed reels).
Come in the gate with a researched plan, then let the data refine it. "We'll figure it out from the data later" is not a day-1 strategy.

## The research (2026 YouTube Shorts — faceless clip + health/longevity niche)

### Algorithm & distribution (the rules of the game)
- A new Short is seeded to a small batch (hundreds–few thousand); the **first 30–60 min** decides whether distribution expands or stops.
- **Swipe-away in the first 1–2 seconds is the dominant signal.** High early swipe-away reads as "content didn't match expectation" → distribution halts almost immediately. Retention from the first frame is the whole game.
- "Good" retention bar ≈ **70% average view duration**.
- **Long discovery tail** — strong Shorts resurface for days/weeks. A consistent schedule at a decent time beats perfect timing applied erratically (the algorithm learns your cadence).

### Posting times & frequency
- **Prime windows: 12–3 PM and 7–9 PM (audience-local).** Tue–Thu / Wed–Fri strongest; weekends can over-index. **Mornings are weak for Shorts** — posting into a sleeping/empty feed gives a weak initial batch → throttle.
- **Frequency: 1/day minimum; 1–2/day is the new-channel sweet spot; 5–7/week for momentum.** Pushing well past ~2/day on a *new* channel can dilute per-post algorithmic attention. Scale volume via MORE CHANNELS, not by cramming one.

### Length
- Health/tip Shorts: **15–30s optimal, 20–25s algorithm-favored, >45s drops off hard.** Cut to the punch, no runway.
- (Conflicting general-Shorts data: 50–60s can maximize completion ~76% — niche-dependent. Bias short for tip/myth content; let A/B settle it.)

### Formats (ranked by what wins)
1. **Myth-busting** — the #1 *shareable* health format (viewers send it to someone who believes the myth).
2. **Actionable, replicable, time-respecting tips** — saves drive reach.
3. **Counterintuitive / shocking claims.**
4. **Research-cited** claims. 5. **Transformations** (immediate visual payoff). Trending: 60+ strength, "realistic/replicable" fitness, longevity routines.

### Hooks (first 3 seconds)
- Visual surprise / bold text / direct question. Lowercase, cue-punctuated, written to THE clip.
- **Citation-in-hook** ("a 2024 study found…", "research shows…") boosts credibility + save rate.
- **85% of Shorts are watched muted** → the burned caption + hook ARE the hook. Captions non-negotiable.

### Monetization (2026)
- YPP entry tier: **500 subs + 3 public posts/90d → 3M valid Shorts views/90d** (or 3k watch hrs). Full YPP: 1k subs + 4k watch hrs OR 10M Shorts views/90d.
- Realistic: **3–6 months** at daily/near-daily posting. Stack AdSense + affiliates + licensing; a viral Short can funnel to long-form (10–50× RPM).

### Mistakes that throttle reach
Dead-window posting · weak first-frame hook / slow open · clips >45s · no captions (muted viewers) · inconsistent schedule · unsafe YMYL claims (clip credentialed experts only).
